# Event 4 Report: GameDay "8 Teams Compete"

### Event Objectives

The **GameDay "8 Teams Compete"** program was organized as an AWS knowledge competition between teams. Instead of only listening to theory, participants could watch teams react, discuss, and answer questions related to AWS topics prepared by the organizers.

Through this event, I had the opportunity to observe how teams approached questions, analyzed situations, and selected suitable answers. This helped me learn more about AWS services and better understand how cloud knowledge can be applied to practical scenarios.

### Main Event Content

In the program, 8 teams competed with each other. The teams answered **10 questions** from one of **10 topics** prepared by the organizers. Each question required participants not only to remember theory but also to understand how AWS services work and how they are used in specific cases.

The interesting part of the program was that each team had to think quickly, cooperate with teammates, and give answers within a limited time. While watching the competition, I could clearly see the difference between learning separate concepts and applying knowledge to answer real scenario-based questions.

The topics were related to the AWS ecosystem and could include service groups such as compute, storage, database, networking, security, monitoring, serverless, and best practices for deploying systems on the cloud. Because of that, viewers could both cheer for the teams and learn new knowledge from each question and explanation.

### What I Observed

While watching the teams compete, I realized that understanding AWS is not only about remembering service names. It is also necessary to understand the purpose, strengths, and limitations of each service. For example, with the same problem, learners need to know when to use a compute service, when storage is needed, which database is suitable, and when security or networking should be considered.

I also noticed the importance of teamwork. Teams needed to discuss quickly, agree on an answer, and make decisions in a short time. This shows that in technology, individual knowledge is important, but collaboration and discussion skills are also necessary.

### What I Learned

#### About AWS Knowledge

- It is important to understand the purpose of each AWS service instead of only memorizing its name.
- AWS services are often combined to solve a complete system problem.
- When choosing a service, it is necessary to consider performance, cost, security, scalability, and reliability.
- Scenario-based questions help me better imagine how AWS is applied in practice.

#### About Problem-Solving Thinking

- Before choosing an answer, I need to read the requirement carefully and identify the problem being solved.
- An AWS question may have several answers that seem close, so understanding the context is important.
- Observing how teams analyzed questions helped me learn a more systematic way of thinking.

#### About Teamwork

- Teamwork helps members support each other when facing difficult questions.
- Quick discussion and agreement are important skills in time-limited activities.
- A team with good coordination can make more accurate decisions.

### Applying The Lessons To Myself

After joining the event, I realized that I should learn AWS by understanding the nature and use cases of each service. Instead of only reading documentation service by service, I should practice more with scenario-based questions, quizzes, and hands-on labs to better understand how AWS services work together.

Besides that, I need to improve my ability to analyze questions and choose suitable solutions. When working on AWS projects or labs, I should ask myself why a service is used, whether there are other options, and whether the solution is suitable in terms of cost, security, and scalability.

### Event Experience

For me, GameDay "8 Teams Compete" was an interesting event because its format created a competitive and exciting atmosphere. Watching teams compete to answer questions made AWS learning less dry and gave me more motivation to study cloud services carefully.

I was especially impressed by how quickly the teams had to react to the questions. Some questions could be confusing if learned only through theory, but after listening to the teams' reasoning and the organizers' explanations, I understood more clearly why one answer was more suitable than another. This was an effective learning method because it was interactive and helped participants remember knowledge better.

### Lessons Learned

- Learning AWS is more effective when theory, scenario-based questions, and hands-on practice are combined.
- It is necessary to understand the purpose of each AWS service to choose the right solution.
- When working with cloud, security, cost, performance, and scalability should be considered.
- Teamwork and quick discussion help solve questions or problems better.
- Activities like GameDay make cloud learning more dynamic and easier to absorb.

### Conclusion

Through the GameDay "8 Teams Compete" program, I had the opportunity to learn AWS knowledge in a more interesting and practical way. Watching teams answer 10 questions from AWS-related topics helped me understand how to analyze questions, choose suitable services, and connect cloud knowledge with real scenarios. The event also reminded me that to learn AWS well, I need to practice actively, work on scenario-based questions, and understand the role of each service in a system.
